Zhongren Chen

I am a Ph.D. student in Statistics and Data Science at Yale University. I am very fortunate to be advised by Joshua Kalla, Johan Ugander, and Xiaohong Chen. My research lies at the intersection of political science, artificial intelligence, and statistics. I examine how AI shapes human behavior and how social science can benefit from the rise of AI. My recent work focuses on:

  • How large language models influence political persuasion and polarization.
  • How machine learning can enhance causal inference under real-world complications.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Statistics from Stanford University, where I was advised by Wing Hung Wong and Lu Tian. I received my B.A. in Mathematics and Statistics from the University of Oxford.
